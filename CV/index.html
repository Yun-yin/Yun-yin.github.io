Species abundance and the structure of interaction network affect the structural stability of forest meta-community -- supervisor: Prof. Yu Mingjian

  • Bachler's Thesis and research exercise project in Chu Kochen Honors college

  • Generated neighborhood relationships using a complete planar graph model; inferred interaction strengths via maximum likelihood under a Markov random field framework

  • Calculated species abundance and structural stability in metacommunity patches, following the Lotka-Volterra model (using linear approximation near equilibrium); applied resampling and null models to assess random effects

  • Found that interaction networks comprising all interacting species in a patch show higher structural stability, while subsets display lower stability, consistent with predictions from structural stability theory
